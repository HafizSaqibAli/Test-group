About Hafiz

Hafiz was born in Hafizabad, Punjab, Pakistan, where he completed his early education before moving to Faisalabad for his BS (Hons) and MPhil degrees. During his master’s studies, he completed one year research project with Prof. Asim Mansha on developing a quantum-mechanics-based model for the design of phthalonitrile derivatives for photodynamic therapy application, which sparked his interest in computational chemistry.

He was awarded a highly competitive Chief Minister Merit Scholarship (CMMS) form the Government of Pakistan, which enabled him to pursue his PhD at the University of Manchester, UK. His doctoral research focused on developing computational algorithms to study reaction kinetics and biomolecular stability. During this time, he developed expertise in multiscale biomolecular modelling, including QM-cluster, QM/MM, and molecular dynamics approaches, enabling the engineering of synthetic heme/non-heme metalloenzymes with novel functions.

Following his PhD, he began his postdoctoral career at the University of Edinburgh, where he applied computational methods to guide the design of Cu-based artificial metalloenzymes. He then moved to the University of Oxford, where he expanded his expertise in free-energy-based computational methods and machine learning for the design of novel small molecules targeting antimicrobial resistance (AMR).

He later joined the University of Strathclyde and GSK, to further his expertise in developing data-driven approaches for drug modalities beyond Lipinski space including Peptides, Oligoneucleotides and PROTACs. Most recently, he was awarded a Liverpool University Research Fellowship and joined the Department of Chemistry and Pharmacology at the University of Liverpool. There, he leads a team developing physics-informed machine learning models to accelerate drug discovery, with a particular focus on bacterial ribosomes to address the growing threat of AMR.
