Research Areas

We advance data-driven molecular design as a platform for solving unmet global challenges — coupling automation, structural insight, and molecular-level understanding to accelerate solutions in human health, sustainable catalysis, and infectious disease. Our group cultivates an environment where rigorous science and bold ideas converge.

⚗️
Area 01

Computational Chemistry

We employ state-of-the-art quantum mechanics/molecular mechanics (QM/MM) calculations, molecular dynamics simulations, and electrostatic modelling to understand enzyme mechanisms at an atomic level.

  • QM/MM studies of enzyme reaction mechanisms
  • Electrostatic perturbation analysis in active sites
  • Free energy calculations and transition state theory
  • Multiscale cell correlation (MCC) methods
  • LogP and partition coefficient prediction

Area 02

Metalloenzymes

We develop novel inhibitors targeting proteins involved in antimicrobial resistance, with a particular focus on β-lactamases, transpeptidases, and deacetylases — aiming to restore the efficacy of existing antibiotics.

  • β-lactamase and transpeptidase inhibitor design
  • Tetracycline destructase (Tet(X)) inhibitors
  • Urease inhibitors targeting H. pylori
  • Structure-activity relationship (SAR) analysis
  • Molecules beyond Lipinski space

Area 03

Machine Learning & Data Science

We develop and apply machine learning models to accelerate molecular design, predict physicochemical properties, and identify novel chemical scaffolds with therapeutic potential.

  • Data-driven molecular property prediction
  • Graph neural networks for molecular design
  • ADMET profiling using ML models
  • Generative models for de novo drug design
  • Integration of experimental and computational data

Area 04

Chemical Biology & Metalloenzymes

We investigate artificial and natural metalloenzymes, combining computational structural modelling with mechanistic insights to enhance catalytic efficiency and inspire the design of new biocatalysts.

  • Artificial metalloenzyme structural modelling
  • Nonheme iron enzyme mechanisms
  • NHC gold(I)-catalysed reactions
  • Ferryl reactivity in oxygenases
  • RNA nuclease activity in CDAs
